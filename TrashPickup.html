Welcome to Trash Pickup!

Here you will find shocking details about the trash that lands up in parks, creeks, and oceans.

Around 90% of all solid waste in the United States is not recycled. All of the trash that lands up in every single type of environment negatively affects the organisms that live their.

Parks and Neighborhoods

Cats and dogs are affected by plastic soda rings that are just left in the streets. Dogs and cats either eat the plastic that isn't recycled, or they can get their heads stuck in one of the holes of a soda ring, which can eventually cut off blood circulation.Not only do this happen, but these pets sufficate on pieces of plastic almost every single day. It only takes 3-5 minutes for a dog or cat to sufficate on a piece of plastc.The most common item that they can sufficate on is snack and chip bags. Lastly, cats and dogs can choke on cereal bags, pet food bags, and pet treat bags.

Creeks

Ducks and other sea birds mistake small pieces of plastic and trash for food. In many cases, this causes the birds to die and even the death of their young. Majority of birds that are taken to a rehab center are affected by fishing lines with hooks, which have pierced through their beaks. Around 1 million birds,specifically sea birds,die each year due to the plastic that ends up around creeks, rivers, lakes, and oceans.

Beach

Hundreds of thousands of sea turtles, whales, and other marine mammals, and more than 1 million seabirds die each year from ocean pollution and ingestion or entanglement in marine debris(This statistic was found from See Turtles.) Sea turtles are extremely affected by the plastic bags and other pieces of plastic that land up in the ocean.The turtles mistake the plastic bags for jellyfish and eat them. After the turtles eat the plastic bags, their disgestive systems are blocked, which can eventually lead to death. Over 1 million marine animals are killed every year due to pieces of plastic that end up in the ocean.

Information

It takes 1-5 years for a cigarette to biodegrade. It takes 2-6 weeks for a paper bag to biodegrade. It takes 80-100 years for an aluminum can to biodegrade. It takes 500 years for a polystyrene cup to biodegrade. It takes 450-1000 years for a plastic bottle to biodegrade. It takes 10-1000 years for a plastic bag to biodegrade.

Conclusion

In conclusion, citizens all over the world need to dispose of their trash properly and recycle more. If we can fix this problem, all types of environments will be clean , amd the organisms that live there can live their lives to the max. Turtles, one of the most majestic, will be able to thrive.
